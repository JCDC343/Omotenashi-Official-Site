In Japanese culture, Omotenashi meaning hospitality, is very significant. Omotenashi is a way of living in Japan, and focuses on providing the best possible services when it comes to hospitality, while also expecting nothing in return. This meaningful concept is what inspired our cafe name. We would like to give our customers the same superb hospitality through omotenashi.

The doodles in which you see decorating our website are called Seigaiha waves. Seigaiha literally means ‘blue sea and waves’. Seigaiha waves are a traditional Japanese design which symbolize power and resistance - key elements of Japanese culture.


Our Values






Polite Courtesies

Cleanliness

In Japan, customers often slurp soup and drinks to let the chef know it tastes delicious! Omotenashi will have chabudai (Japanese tables) which are traditionally low and requires customers to kneel while having meals.

Keeping areas clean at all times is also an important practice for Omotenashi as one of Japan's main religions, Shintoism, conducts the rituals of purity and purification frequently.

Hospitality

At Omotenashi Café, Hospitality is our main value. Our staff aim to always treat customers with kindness and provide as much as we can for a diverse, comfy, and cute environment for our customers.
